Hello! My name is Cameron Kucin, and I am currently a freshman at North Carolina State University with an intended Computer Science major. What got me interested in computer science was a senior project I did for my advanced game art & design class in high school. The class was divided into several small teams that would each go on to create their own unique, original, and playable games in Unity using the skills we had learned up to that point. On my team, I ended up being the one working inside the Unity game engine the most, assembling environments together, creating interfaces, and doing the majority of the project's programming. It was this programming role that really sparked my interest in coding, despite not having the easiest of starts in C#.



Despite having worked on a team developed game in the past in addition to currently leading the development of a second project, I don't know if working in the game industry would be my dream job. Sure, I enjoy doing it and getting to be a part of the development process, but given how competitive the industry is as well as how intense time crunches are in real game development settings, I'm just not sure if that sort of workplace is one that I'd want to be a part of in the long-term. I'm still interested in learning more programming languages, and I'm also open to going into fields related to nuclear fusion and brain anatomy. However, I think it's far too early for me to decide on a dream career path.
